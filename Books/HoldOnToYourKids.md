# Hold On to your Kids
**Gordon Neufeld, Ph.D., Gabor Maté**

## The Six Ways of Attaching
1. Senses: Physical proximity (haptic, sound, smell, visual)
2. Sameness: Imitation and emulation ("Be the person you want your kids to become")
3. Belongin and Loyalty: Laying claim to whomeve/whatever they feel attached to. This also gets disrupted when the first-born gets a sibling and suddenly has to share time/engery/attention from the parents.
4. Significance: Children want to please and get praise/approval from whom they are attached to. They want to share their creations/drawings/achievments
5. Feeling: Love, affection and other emotional intimacy. Children who have eomtional intimacy with parents can bear physcial separation better.
6. Being known: Sharing secrets, gossip, Being seen and heard psychologically
